Rosalind Franklin

The scientist whose shadow long concealed the light

Introduction

Discover the story of an exceptional researcher whose role in the discovery of DNA was long underestimated.

A pioneering woman in crystallography

Born in 1920, Rosalind Franklin was an expert in X-ray diffraction... Her famous "Photo 51" was a major step in the discovery of the double helix.

"Science and everyday life cannot be separated." - Rosalind Franklin

The Matilda Effect: The shadow cast on her discoveries

This phenomenon refers to the systematic minimization of the contributions of women scientists. Franklin, like many others, was deprived of the recognition she deserved for her work.

Popularization of the Theory

The Matilda effect refers to a phenomenon where the contributions of women scientists are minimized, ignored, or wrongly attributed... This theory was formalized in the 1990s by historian Margaret Rossiter.

BU Object

In this type of book, mainly men like Newton, Maxwell, or Einstein are cited, while women scientists remain almost invisible. Yet in the history of physics, it is estimated that most discoveries are due to women including Rosalind Franklin for the structure of DNA.
